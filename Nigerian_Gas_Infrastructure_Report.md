# Nigerian Domestic Gas Pipeline Infrastructure and Mid/Downstream Assets - Comprehensive Report

**Report Date:** July 2026
**Compiled for:** NNPC Gas Platform Project

---

## Executive Summary

Nigeria operates over 3,200 km of gas pipelines with three major transmission networks:
1. **Eastern Network:** Alakiri-Obigbo-Ikot Abasi Pipeline System
2. **Western Network:** Escravos-Lagos Pipeline System (ELPS)
3. **Northern Network:** Ajaokuta-Kaduna-Kano (AKK) Pipeline (under development)

**Current Production:** ~7.5-7.93 bcf/d (2025-2026)
**Domestic Supply:** ~2,000-2,200 MMscf/d to power and industrial sectors
**Production Target:** 10 bcf/d by 2027, 12 bcf/d by 2030

---

## 1. DOMESTIC GAS PIPELINES

### 1.1 ESCRAVOS-LAGOS PIPELINE SYSTEM (ELPS) - Western Network

**Operator:** Nigerian Gas Processing and Transportation Company (NGPTC)
**Status:** Operational since 1989
**Route:** Escravos → Utorogu → Warri → Egbin (Lagos) → Itoki

#### Technical Specifications:
- **Main Line (Warri to Egbin):** 36 inches (910 mm) diameter
- **Escravos to Warri:** 24 inches (610 mm) diameter
- **Egbin Tee to Alagbado:** 24 inches (610 mm) diameter
- **Feeder Lines:**
  - ELPS A: 20-24 inches from Escravos
  - ELPS B: 30 inches from Utorogu
  - Main ELPS: 36 inches delivery to Itoki
- **Capacity:** 800 MMscf/d (original); 2,200 MMscf/d (after ELPS II expansion completed 2019)
- **Length:** Approximately 340 km

#### Key Coordinates:
- **Escravos Gas Plant:** 5.6113°N, 5.2010°E (Delta State)
- **Warri Gas Treatment Plant:** ~5.5442°N, 5.7603°E (Delta State)
- **Lagos (Itoki Terminal):** ~6.6500°N, 3.3500°E

#### Major Supply Points:
- Escravos Gas Plant (Chevron, 680 MMscf/d)
- Utorogu Gas Plant (ND Western/NPDC, 300 MMscf/d)
- Odidi Gas Plant (Neconde/NPDC, 80-300 MMscf/d)
- Oben Gas Plant (Seplat, 525 MMscf/d)

#### Power Plants Supplied:
- Egbin Power Station (Lagos) - 1,320 MW
- Delta/Ughelli Power Station (Delta) - 942 MW
- Sapele Power Station (Delta) - 450 MW
- Omotosho I & II (Ondo) - 785 MW combined
- Olorunsogo I & II (Ogun) - 1,010 MW combined
- Ihovbor/NIPP (Edo) - capacity TBD

#### Industrial Off-takers:
- Warri Refinery & Petrochemicals (Ekpan, Uvwie)
- West African Portland Cement (WAPCO) - Shagamu & Ewekoro
- Industries at Ikorodu
- City Gate Ikeja, Lagos

### 1.2 OBIAFU-OBRIKOM-OBEN (OB3) PIPELINE - East-West Connector

**Operator:** Nigerian Gas Company (NGC) / NGPTC
**Status:** Operational (River Niger crossing completed April 2026)
**Route:** Obiafu-Obrikom (Rivers State) → Oben (Edo State)

#### Technical Specifications:
- **Diameter:** 48 inches
- **Length:** 127-130 km
- **Capacity:** 2,000 MMscf/d (2 bcf/d)
- **Investment:** ~$700 million
- **Special Feature:** River Niger crossing via horizontal directional drilling (2 km beneath riverbed)

#### Purpose:
Connects Eastern gas supply sources (Rivers State) with Western demand centers, serving as critical East-West pipeline backbone.

#### Key Nodes:
- **Obiafu-Obrikom Gas Plant:** Rivers State (near Omoku)
- **Oben Gas Processing Plant:** ~5.7°N, 5.9°E, Delta/Edo border (Seplat, 525 MMscf/d)

---

### 1.3 OBEN-GEREGU PIPELINE - Northern Supply Route

**Operator:** NGPTC/NGC
**Status:** Operational (completed 2011)
**Route:** Oben (Edo) → Ajaokuta Steel Complex (Kogi) → Geregu (Kogi)

#### Technical Specifications:
- **Diameter:** 36 inches
- **Length:** 196 km
- **Capacity:** 1,200 MMscf/d (1.2 bcf/d)
- **Construction Cost:** $228.3 million
- **Tariff:** $0.8/MMscf

#### Power Plants Supplied:
- **Geregu I:** 414 MW (open cycle gas turbine)
- **Geregu II (NIPP):** 434 MW (simple cycle)
- **Total Geregu Capacity:** 848 MW

#### Strategic Importance:
- Feeds Ajaokuta Steel Company
- Serves as trunk for future AKK pipeline connection
- Critical for northern gas supply

---

### 1.4 ALAKIRI-OBIGBO-IKOT ABASI PIPELINE - Eastern Network

**Operator:** NGPTC
**Status:** Operational
**Route:** Alakiri (Rivers) → Obigbo (Rivers) → Ikot Abasi (Akwa Ibom)

#### Technical Specifications:
- **Diameter:** 14/16/24 inches (varying sections)
- **Length:** 117 km (73 miles)
- **Capacity:** 1.5 billion cubic meters per year (~395 MMscf/d)
- **Area Covered:** Eastern Niger Delta

#### Purpose:
Serves industrial customers and power plants in eastern Nigeria, connecting gas fields in Rivers State to Akwa Ibom.

---

### 1.5 AJAOKUTA-KADUNA-KANO (AKK) PIPELINE - Northern Network

**Operator:** NNPC Limited
**Status:** Under Construction (First gas expected July 2026 to Abuja)
**Route:** Ajaokuta (Kogi) → Abuja → Kaduna → Zaria → Kano

#### Technical Specifications:
- **Diameter:** 40 inches (sections: 40"x303km documented)
- **Total Length:** 614 km
  - Ajaokuta to Abuja: 200+ km
  - Abuja to Kaduna: 193 km
  - Kaduna to Zaria: 97 km
  - Zaria to Kano: 124 km
- **Capacity:** 3,500 MMscf/d (3.5 bcf/d) design; 2,200 MMscf/d (2.2 bcf/d) Phase 1
- **Investment:** $2.8 billion
- **First Conceived:** 2008
- **Construction Started:** 2020

#### Infrastructure:
- Terminal Gas Stations (TGS) at: Ajaokuta, Abuja, Kaduna, Kano
- Block Valve Stations
- Metering Stations
- Pigging Stations
- Compressor Stations (Obigo compressor station documented)

#### Strategic Importance:
- Phase 1 of Trans-Nigeria Gas Pipeline (TNGP) project
- Opens northern markets for domestic gas
- Supports industrial development in northern Nigeria
- CNG supply to Abuja and northern cities

## CONCLUSION

Nigeria's domestic gas infrastructure is anchored by three major pipeline networks (ELPS/Western, Alakiri-Obigbo-Ikot Abasi/Eastern, and AKK/Northern under development) with total operational capacity exceeding 5,000 MMscf/d when fully utilized. The system supplies approximately 2,000-2,200 MMscf/d to domestic power generation (primary consumer) and industrial sectors as of 2026.

Key recent milestones include:
- OB3 East-West connector completion (April 2026, 2,000 MMscf/d)
- ANOH plant commissioning (January 2026, 300 MMscf/d)
- AKK pipeline first gas delivery to Abuja (July 2026 expected)
- Strategic gas supply agreements with Dangote Group (February 2026)

The sector requires $22 billion in new investments (Gas Master Plan 2026) to achieve production targets of 10 bcf/d by 2027 and 12 bcf/d by 2030, focusing on connecting stranded reserves, expanding transmission capacity, and developing last-mile distribution infrastructure.
